A great success of "Rocky"

Real inspiration for Stallone

In the 70s, when Muhammad Ali as a heavyweight world champion achieved numerous successes in the ring and was second to none, boxing was the most popular among all combat sports. On March 24, 1975, Sylvester Stallone watched the fight for the heavyweight world championship Muhammad Ali versus Chuck Wepner. Ali was then the unquestioned champion defending the title, and Wepner a pretender who all the fans condemned to a quick defeat. However, Wepner withstood 15 rounds and almost knocked out the Master. Stallone liked the heroic attitude of the pretender and decided to shoot a sports drama.

Oscars...and more awards

The film won Golden Globe for Best Drama, but the list of awards doesn't end there. This masterpiece dominated the Oscars in 1976, and won in three categories: Best Film, Best Director - John G. Avildsen and Best Editing - Richard Halsey and Scott Conrad. The movie also got seven nominations: Best Leading Actor - Sylvester Stallone, Best Actress Starring - Talia Shire, Best Supporting Actor - Burgess Meredith, Best Supporting Actor - Burt Young, Best Original Screenplay - Sylvester Stallone, Best Song - Gonna Fly Now performed by Deetta West and Nelson Pigford and Best Sound - Bud Alper, Harry W. Tetrick, Lyle J. Burbridge and William L. McCaughey.
